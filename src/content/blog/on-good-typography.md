Good typography is mostly invisible. You notice bad type immediately, but great type
just feels *right*. A few things I always check:

## Hierarchy

Every screen should answer "where do I look first?" in under a second. Size, weight,
and spacing do that work — not color alone.

## Measure

Line length matters more than people think. Aim for **45–75 characters** per line for
body text. Too wide and the eye gets lost returning to the next line.

## Rhythm

Consistent vertical spacing creates a calm, intentional feel. Pick a spacing scale and
stick to it everywhere.

That's it. Small details, big difference.
